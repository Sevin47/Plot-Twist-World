What is this?

Think Monopoly played on the actual Earth, with the rules of an idle game. You buy real-world parcels, they earn rent every second whether you're online or not, you build them up from an empty lot to a tower, and you trade — or fight — with real other players in one persistent, shared world. There's no fictional continent here: if you zoom in on your own street, it's really there, classified by whatever it actually is — downtown, waterfront, suburb, open water.

No real money, ever This was a design rule from day one. ₲ can't be bought, sold, or cashed out. The "boost ads" you watch in-game are parody brands with joke names — nothing behind them is a real advertiser or a real transaction.
Did you know The map isn't a stylized approximation — every tile's classification is derived live from real OpenStreetMap water, land-use, and building-footprint data for that exact spot on Earth. Nobody hand-placed a single one; the game just reads what's really there.

Getting started

From opening the app for the first time to owning your first tile.

01
Sign in with Google
One account per player, tied to your Google sign-in. There's no separate "new game" — this is it, permanently.
02
Pick a username
2–16 letters, numbers, or underscores. Permanent for the account, and what other players see on your deeds, listings, chat, and the leaderboard.
03
Choose your starting area
A basemap-only screen — pan and zoom the real map to find your home turf, then drop the pin.
04
Claim it & learn the ropes
Confirming rolls your first tile's rarity, claims it free, and sets that region as your permanent home. A short interactive tutorial follows, covering the basics live on your own deed.

From there you're in the full game: the map, the four tabs (Map / Assets / Market / HQ), and the whole economy described below.

The interface

Four tabs along the bottom, plus a persistent header.

Map
The world itself — pan, zoom, buy, build, attack, and chat with whoever shares your region.
Assets
Every tile you own, filterable and sortable. Tiles under construction get their own dedicated section up top, sorted soonest-to-finish, separate from the rest of your portfolio.
Market
Player listings inside territory you've unlocked. See The open market.
HQ
Your account: territory, the world register (leaderboard), Friends, activity log, and account settings.

The header shows your balance, income rate, status rank, energy remaining, attacks remaining, and how many tiles are currently under construction — the whole account state at a glance, on every tab.

Reading the map

The map shows different levels of detail depending on zoom — and how much of the world you've actually unlocked.

Wide zoom — basemap
Real map imagery with place labels. Free to explore anywhere on Earth, no restrictions.
Mid zoom — classification preview
A coarse color wash shows classification tiers across whatever region you're looking at. Still just browsing.
Deep zoom — the grid
Individual ~306 m tiles become visible and tappable. This is where you buy, build, attack, and trade.

Territory and fog

Once you own at least one tile, the interactive grid only lights up inside regions you've unlocked (roughly 150 km chunks of the world). Everywhere else appears fogged on the grid — you can still browse it at wide zoom, but you can't buy, attack, chat, or trade there until you unlock that region. Your home region is always free. See Regions & unlocking for cost.

How a spot gets classified

  • If it's within roughly 150 m of open water, it becomes Waterfront (dense) or Coast (sparse).
  • Otherwise, real building-footprint density decides the tier: Downtown at ≥62% built-up coverage, Urban at ≥40%, Suburbs at ≥18%, Rural below that.
  • Explicit OpenStreetMap land-use tags (commercial, residential, industrial, park, farmland, etc.) override the density guess where they exist.
Surveying… If a spot hasn't finished loading real map data yet, it shows as "Surveying…" — not purchasable. This is honest uncertainty, not a guess. Give it a moment and it resolves to a real classification.
Did you know Price and rent scale together so tightly that classification barely affects your rent-per-₲-invested — Downtown edges out the rest at 0.281 rps per ₲1,000, Suburbs trails at 0.25. The real choice is how much capital you want tied up in one tile, not which classification is "best." See Starter strategies.

Rarity

Every tile is rolled a rarity the moment you buy it — pure luck, not something you can choose or influence.

RarityOddsRent multiplier
Common70%×1
Uncommon20%×1.5
Rare8%×3
Legendary2%×8

Rarity applies for as long as you own that specific tile and multiplies its rent directly. Selling a tile or losing it in combat loses the rarity roll with it — whoever ends up owning it next (via purchase, listing, or capture) keeps whatever rarity is already on the tile; only a brand-new claim on previously-unowned land rolls fresh. Landmark tiles never roll rarity — they're always Common, on purpose, so the incentive to hold one is the perk and the trophy, not a lucky multiplier.

Buying land & energy

Claiming a brand-new, never-owned tile is gated by energy — a daily allowance, not a wallet check.

Energy is the real limiter on how fast the map gets claimed — not how much ₲ you have. Every claim of a new, unowned tile costs 1 energy. Trading with other players and upgrading tiles you already own never cost energy.

Hard daily cap — no banking Energy resets once per day at midnight UTC, to whatever your current status rank grants. Unused energy does not carry over — a day you don't spend it is a day it's gone, not saved up.

Claiming your very first tile ever (via the starting-area picker) is always free and unrestricted — no region-unlock needed, no energy spent. Every claim after that follows the normal rules.

Buildings & construction

A tile starts as a vacant lot and can be built up through four levels — and building takes real, elapsed time.

LV 3
Apartments
2 hours to build.
LV 4
Tower
8 hours to build. Maxed — the only level that can be redeveloped.

Rent scales directly with level: a Tower earns 5× the tile's base rent (level counts as 1 + level). Starting an upgrade begins a real countdown — the tile keeps earning its current rent while it builds, then jumps to the new rent the moment it finishes.

Builder slots

How many tiles can be under construction at once is capped by your status rank — 2 slots at Squatter, rising to 6 at Mogul. Starting an upgrade on a tile beyond your slot count while all your slots are full simply isn't possible until one finishes.

Rushing a build

Impatient? Rushing pays the remaining fraction of the full upgrade cost to finish instantly — rush a build that's 90% done and you're only paying for the last 10%, not the whole thing again. See Formula reference for the exact math.

Upgrade All

From the Assets tab, Upgrade All starts a build on every eligible, affordable tile (cheapest first) until your builder slots or balance run out. Anything it starts appears in the dedicated Building section at the top of Assets, so you can see exactly what it did.

Naming your tiles

Give any tile you own a private nickname — useful once your portfolio grows past a handful of coordinates that all start to look alike.

Tap the ✎ next to a tile's name in its sheet to set one, up to 24 characters. It's private to you — nobody else, including whoever owned the tile before you or buys/captures it after you, ever sees it. It also shows up in the Assets tab in place of the raw coordinates, and Assets search matches nicknames too.

Resets on transfer A nickname is cleared automatically the moment a tile changes hands — sold on the market, captured in a raid, or repossessed for inactivity. The next owner starts with a blank slate to name it themselves, the same as you did.

Every tile also has a short, permanent, non-editable ID (the last 6 characters of its map coordinate string) shown in both the sheet and the Assets list — two nearby tiles can round to the same displayed coordinates, or even end up with the same nickname, so this ID is what actually guarantees you're looking at the right one.

Redeveloping (prestige)

Once a tile is maxed out at Tower, tear it down for a permanent bonus instead of just sitting on it.

Redevelop resets a Tower back to a Vacant lot — instantly, for free — and grants a permanent +25% rent bonus on that tile, stacking with every previous cycle. There's no hard limit on how many times you can redevelop the same tile.

Redevelop is free — rebuilding isn't The reset itself costs nothing. What gets more expensive is climbing back to Tower afterward: each redevelop cycle raises both the ₲ cost and the time to rebuild through all four levels again. That penalty caps out after 10 cycles — the rent bonus keeps growing forever, but cost and build time stop climbing.

Redevelop keeps the tile — you still own it, you're just resetting the building to earn a permanent multiplier. There is no way to cash a tile out for a lump sum in the current version; see the note in The open market.

Abandoning a tile

Give up any tile you own instantly for a partial refund.

Abandoning refunds 50% of everything you've paid into that tile — purchase price plus every upgrade — and releases it back to the world, unowned. Quick, simple, always available. Useful for cutting losses on a tile that isn't earning its keep, or one that's become a PvP liability.

Landmarks

24 real-world landmarks, 9 tiles each (216 total worldwide) — expensive, rarity-free, and the source of the game's only non-income perks.

LandmarksPerkValue per tileCap
Eiffel Tower, Sagrada Família, Neuschwanstein, Tokyo Tower, CN Tower, Space NeedleBuild speed−2% build time30%
Great Wall, Colosseum, Kremlin, Petra, Machu Picchu, Angkor WatSiege discount−2% attack cost30%
Statue of Liberty, Golden Gate Bridge, Burj Khalifa, Mount Rushmore, Table Mountain, AcropolisEnergy boost+2 energy/daynone
Big Ben, Taj Mahal, Great Pyramid, Sydney Opera House, Christ the Redeemer, Leaning Tower of PisaRush discount−2% rush cost30%
  • Claim price: a flat ₲1,000,000 per tile, regardless of which landmark or real-world classification it would otherwise have.
  • No rarity roll — always Common. The reward is the perk and the trophy, not a lucky multiplier.
  • Defense multiplier: ×1.5 defense power against attacks — landmark tiles are meaningfully harder to take than an ordinary tile of the same level.
  • 48-hour grace period: a landmark tile can't be attacked for 48 hours after it changes owner (by any means), so a fresh claim isn't immediately sieged back.
  • Perks are region-scoped (build speed, siege discount, rush discount only count landmark tiles you own in the same region as the tile being upgraded/attacked/rushed) — except energy boost, which is account-wide, since energy itself isn't tied to any one region.

Perk value stacks per tile owned — own all 9 tiles of one landmark and a percentage-based perk reaches 18% (under its 30% cap); an energy-boost landmark's full set adds a flat +18 energy/day.

PvP & attacks

Take a tile from another player by force — but only from your own border.

Adjacency

You can only attack a tile if you already own at least one tile orthogonally adjacent to it (north, south, east, or west — not diagonal). This keeps PvP a contiguous frontier, not a random snipe from across the planet.

Daily limits

Attacks you may launch per day scale with your status rank — 6/day at Squatter, up to 20/day at Mogul (see the status table for every rank in between). Attacks a single tile may absorb per day is flat for everyone: 2, regardless of either side's rank.

The two caps are independent — a well-defended tile can only be hit twice a day regardless of who's attacking it, but your daily attack allowance lets you try elsewhere the moment one target is tapped out, rather than losing the rest of your day.

Cost & odds

Attack cost is the greater of a base formula (half the target's price, scaling with its level) or 0.2% of your own peak net worth — whichever is higher. See Formula reference for the exact numbers. Win probability is your attack power (how many owned tiles border the target) versus the defender's power (their level, rarity, prestige, and any landmark defense bonus), clamped between 5% and 90% either way — no attack is ever a sure thing, and no defense is ever unbeatable.

Losing costs nothing extra A failed attack still costs the attack fee — that's the whole risk. A successful attack transfers the tile outright, resetting any build in progress; a successful defense costs the defender nothing and keeps any build running.
Did you know The wealth floor (0.2% of peak net worth) means a brand-new account's attacks are priced almost entirely by the base formula — the floor only starts to bite once your peak net worth climbs into real money. Punching up is always cheap; punching down gets expensive as you get rich.

Winning a raid and capturing a tile unlocks the Conqueror achievement. New players and accounts less than 72 hours old can't be attacked at all.

The open market

A live, player-driven trading post.

Any tile you own can be listed at any price you choose. Sales pay you instantly, even while you're offline — the buyer gets exactly what you listed: your level, rarity, and prestige, unchanged.

Only your unlocked territory The Market only shows — and only lets you buy — tiles inside regions you've already unlocked, the same boundary the map's fog draws for browsing. Unlock a new region to trade there too.
Removed feature An earlier version of the game let players "flip" a maxed-out tile for an instant, formula-priced payout instead of listing it. It was cut — Abandoning already covers "I want out now," and listing already covers "I want to sell on my own terms," so flip didn't carry its own weight as a third option. If you're reading an older guide or screenshot that mentions flipping, it no longer exists.

Leaderboard

The top 10 players by current net worth (balance + total invested across all owned tiles), visible to everyone from HQ.

Unlike status rank, the leaderboard reflects your current standing, not your peak — it moves up or down as players buy, sell, and build. Your status rank badge shows next to your name here too.

World land claimed

A live counter on the map showing how much of the world's purchasable land has been claimed, worldwide, by everyone.

The denominator isn't a guess — it's computed from real coastline data (Natural Earth's 1:50m land polygons), reprojected into the exact same map projection the game's tile grid uses and clipped to the same reachable latitude band, so it accounts for how that projection distorts area at different latitudes. The result: about 38.35% of the reachable grid is land, versus the ~29.2% you'd get from Earth's real spherical surface area — projected map space and real surface area aren't the same thing, and using the wrong one would have undercounted by nearly a third.

Did you know Almost all of Antarctica sits outside the game's coordinate system entirely — the map projection is clipped at about ±85.05° latitude (the same limit real-world slippy maps use), so there's no tile coordinate for most of the deep south. It isn't excluded on purpose; there's simply nowhere to put it.

Friends

Add other players by username from HQ. Costs nothing, and their territory lights up teal on the map.

Send a request by exact landlord name; they can accept, decline, or block. Sending each other a request at the same time auto-accepts instead of creating two pending requests. From your friends list you can jump straight to a friend's home region with one tap. Making your first friend unlocks the Sociable achievement.

Rate limit Up to 20 pending friend requests may be sent per day.

Direct messages

Private, friends-only chat — you can't be messaged by anyone you haven't accepted.

Open a conversation from any friend's row in HQ. Unread counts show per-friend and as a dot on the HQ tab. Sending a first message unlocks the Pen pal achievement.

Limits Up to 20 messages per minute, per account. Each conversation keeps its most recent 200 messages.

Region chat

Local chat scoped to whichever region you're currently looking at — you talk to the people whose territory you actually share.

Open it from the speech-bubble button on the map. The room follows your camera live — pan into a different unlocked region and the header, the message list, and where your next message goes all switch the instant you arrive, with a small notification confirming the new room. No need to close and reopen it to "refresh." Locked regions show a hint to unlock first rather than an empty room. Each region is named after the nearest real place close enough to be recognizable, or by coordinates when nothing nearby has a name.

Limits Up to 10 messages per minute, per account. Each region keeps its most recent 100 messages.

Reporting & blocking

Every message, anywhere, can be reported or its sender blocked.

Tap a message in region chat to reveal Report and Block. Blocking is mutual and immediate — a blocked player can no longer message you, send you friend requests, or appear in your region chat, and you disappear from theirs the same way. Blocks can be lifted any time from the Blocked list in HQ's Friends card.

Reports are private — only visible to the game's operator, never to other players, including the person you reported. Up to 10 reports may be filed per day.

Tutorial

A short, five-step interactive walkthrough for brand-new accounts.

It starts automatically right after your first claim, once the welcome animations settle, and covers the core loop live on your own tile: what you just claimed, starting a build, zooming out to see your territory, and what energy is for. Two of the five steps advance automatically once you actually do the thing they're teaching, not just by clicking Next. It can be skipped at any point, and replayed any time from HQ's account section if you want a refresher.

Daily streak

Log in once per day (UTC) to claim a stipend that grows with consecutive days.

Daily reward₲150 × min(current streak, 7)

The reward climbs each day through day 7 (₲1,050), then holds flat at ₲1,050/day for every day after — a day-100 streak pays the same as a day-7 streak. Missing a single day resets your streak back to 1. A 3-day streak unlocks the Regular achievement.

Offline earnings

Rent keeps accruing while you're away, at two different rates depending on exactly how long it's been.

Actively synced (≤2 min)
Full rent rate. A boost's 2× multiplier only applies inside this window.
Away (>2 min)
Half rate, capped at 8 hours worth — a week-long trip earns the same as an 8-hour gap, not 20× more.
Did you know Boosts don't apply retroactively to offline time — the 2× multiplier only counts while you were actively syncing (within 2 minutes of your last check-in). Popping a boost right before closing the tab doesn't boost the hours you're away.

Boosts

A free, parody "watch an ad" mechanic for a temporary rent multiplier.

Tapping the boost button (fake ad, joke brand, no real ad network involved) grants 2× rent for 5 minutes, only while actively playing (see Offline earnings). It's on a 30-minute cooldown between uses — long enough to be something to catch, not a timer to babysit.

Inactivity & repossession

If an account goes untouched for 30+ days, its tiles are gradually returned to the world a few at a time, with a 30% refund credited to that account and a notification waiting on next login. This keeps abandoned land from sitting locked forever in a world where every tile is one-of-one — it's not a punishment, just how a shared, finite world stays healthy. Repossessed tiles go back to genuinely unclaimed status — anyone can buy them fresh, no adjacency or region-unlock shortcuts.

Starter strategies

A few different viable approaches to the early game, grounded in the actual formulas above rather than folk wisdom. None of these are mandatory or mutually exclusive.

The Daily GrindBest for: everyone
Energy doesn't bank and a missed day resets your streak all the way to 1. The single highest-value habit in the game is logging in once every day, spending your energy the same day, and collecting the stipend before it caps at day 7. This alone outperforms most "clever" plays over a long enough timeline.
Rural SpamBest for: brand-new accounts
At ₲50 a tile, a Squatter's 10 daily energy can claim 10 Rural tiles for ₲500 total — comfortably inside most starting balances. Spreading your first day's energy across many cheap footholds compounds rent immediately, instead of saving toward one Downtown tile and leaving most of that day's energy unspent.
Classification Doesn't Matter (Yet)Best for: capital allocation
Rent-per-₲-invested is nearly flat across classifications (0.25–0.28 per ₲1,000 — see Property classification), and upgrade cost scales off the same base price, so the real decision isn't "which classification" but "how much capital do I want locked in one tile." Many cheap tiles spread your exposure to a bad PvP day across more surface area; one expensive tile concentrates it.
Punch Up While You're SmallBest for: early-to-mid PvP
Attack cost is whichever is higher: the base formula, or 0.2% of your peak net worth. A new account's peak net worth is near zero, so that floor barely applies yet — your attacks are about as cheap as they'll ever be. As your net worth grows, so does the floor, and PvP gets structurally more expensive. If raiding appeals to you at all, it's cheapest before you're established, not after.
Treat Landmarks as a Late-Game ProjectBest for: established accounts
A flat ₲1,000,000 per tile with no rarity roll and a 48-hour attack grace period is not a starter buy. Landmark perks are account-shaping utility (build speed, cheaper sieges, cheaper rushes, more energy), not raw income, and they only get meaningful once you own several tiles of the same landmark. Save them for once your economy can absorb a seven-figure purchase without blinking.
Make Friends on Day OneBest for: everyone, costs nothing
Friends cost nothing to add, light up teal on the map so you can see their footprint from orbit, and unlock DMs. Region chat puts you in the same room as whoever else has unlocked your territory. There's no direct mechanical bonus, but a persistent shared world is a lot stickier with people you actually know in it — and it's the cheapest strategy on this list.

Formula reference

Every formula that drives the economy, exactly as the game computes it. l = building level (0–4), r = rarity multiplier, pr = prestige cycles, price = the tile's base classification price.

Rent per second

rent = base_rps × rarity_mult × (1 + level) × (1 + 0.25 × prestige)

Upgrade cost

cost = round( price × 0.8 × (level + 1)1.6 × (1 + 0.5 × min(prestige, 10)) )

Build duration

seconds = base_seconds[target level] × (1 + 0.25 × min(prestige, 10)) × (1 − landmark_build_speed% / 100) base_seconds: Lv1 = 300 (5 min) · Lv2 = 1,800 (30 min) · Lv3 = 7,200 (2 h) · Lv4 = 28,800 (8 h)

Rush cost

cost = ceil( upgrade_cost × (seconds_remaining / total_seconds) × (1 − landmark_rush_discount% / 100) )

Redevelop

level → 0, prestige → prestige + 1 (no direct ₲ cost)

Region unlock cost

cost = 1,000 × 2 ^ (paid regions already unlocked)

Attack cost

cost = max( round( target_price × 0.5 × (1 + 0.5 × target_level) × (1 − landmark_siege_discount% / 100) ), round( attacker_peak_net_worth × 0.002 ) )

Defense power

power = (1 + level) × rarity_mult × (1 + 0.25 × prestige) × landmark_defense_mult (×1.5 if applicable)

Attack power & win chance

attack_power = number of tiles you own orthogonally adjacent to the target win_chance = clamp( attack_power / (attack_power + defense_power), 5%, 90% )

Refunds

abandon refund = 50% of everything paid into the tile repossession refund = 30% of everything paid, after 30+ days inactive

Daily streak

reward = ₲150 × min(current streak, 7)

Offline earnings

if time since last sync ≤ 2 minutes: rent × (2 if boosted else 1) × elapsed seconds else: rent × 0.5 × min(elapsed seconds, 8 hours)

Landmark perks

build_speed / siege_discount / rush_discount: −2% per tile owned, capped at 30% (region-scoped) energy_boost: +2 energy/day per tile owned (account-wide, uncapped)

The game auto-detects new deploys and prompts a refresh; a "What's new" popup and version badge live in the header. This wiki reflects v1.9.0. Recent highlights:

v1.9.0Jul 21, 2026
  • Tiles can now be given a private nickname — visible only to you, cleared automatically if the tile is ever sold or captured.
  • Assets list and the tile sheet now always show a short permanent ID too, so tiles with similar names or coordinates are never ambiguous.
  • UI text and spacing now scale up on large monitors (1600px+, and again past 2200px) — previously fixed-size everywhere, which read as tiny on 32"+ screens.
  • Reworded the tutorial's upgrade step after player feedback that it wasn't clear how to actually reach the Upgrade button.
v1.8.0Jul 21, 2026
  • Tile decay shortened from 60 to 30 days of inactivity.
  • Status ranks: Magnate's threshold raised 2M → 10M, plus two new ranks above it (Tycoon, Mogul).
  • Daily attack allowance now scales with status rank instead of being flat for everyone.
  • Region chat rebuilt to follow the camera live — no more closing and reopening it to switch rooms.
  • Region names are now far more accurate — real nearby places instead of "unnamed territory" for most of the world.
v1.7.1Jul 21, 2026
  • Replaced "How to play" with a direct Wiki link on the sign-in screen.
v1.7.0Jul 21, 2026
  • This wiki is now deployed alongside the game itself, linked from the sign-in screen and HQ.
v1.6.2Jul 21, 2026
  • Fixed the Assets Building section reordering/flickering during Upgrade All.
v1.6.1Jul 21, 2026
  • Daily attack cap doubled, 3 → 6.
  • Assets tab: dedicated Building section.
v1.6.0Jul 20, 2026
  • Region chat, plus report and block.
v1.5.0Jul 20, 2026
  • Friend direct messages.
v1.4.0Jul 20, 2026
  • Friends: requests, blocking, teal territory on the map.
v1.3.0 – v1.3.1Jul 20, 2026
  • Interactive first-run tutorial, replayable from HQ.
  • "District" renamed to "property classification" throughout.
v1.2.0 – v1.2.2Jul 20, 2026
  • World land-claimed indicator, later corrected to a real coastline-based estimate.
  • Fixed a memory-leak crash after sustained play in dense areas.
v1.1.0Jul 20, 2026
  • World view shows your territory in blue, rivals in red.
  • Update-available banner and changelog system.
